# Personal Guide to Healthy Weight Gain

> 
> 1. **Eat every 3-4 hours**, aiming for **3 main meals** and **2-3 snacks** per day. Eat a snack after 1 hour of eating a main meal
> 2. Take note of the foods to eat and not eat below
> 3. Exercise 2 to 3 times per week
>

# Weight Gain Basics

Weight gain happens when you regularly consume more energy (calories) than your body uses, known as a calorie surplus. 

Combining a calorie-surplus weight gain diet with strength or resistance training helps ensure that a greater share of the added weight is lean muscle rather than only body fat.

# Foods to Include

**1. Nutrient-Dense Protein**

- For lean meats: **chicken** and the meaty part of **beef** (not fat basically)
- Fatty fish: **tuna**, salmon, mackerel
- **Eggs** - eat daily
- Dairy foods: **milk, cheese, yogurt**
- Plant-based proteins: chickpeas and **tofu**


**2. Healthy Fats**

- Healthy fats: **avocados, nuts, and seeds** (almonds, walnuts, and **chia seeds**)
	- For chia seeds, soak them in water for 7 minutes first
- Nut butters: **peanut butter** and almond butter
- Healthy oils: **olive oil and coconut oil** can be drizzled over food

	These fats improve heart health, support brain function, and enhance nutrient absorption, making them a vital component of a balanced nutrition plan for weight gain.


**3. Complex Carbohydrates**

- **White rice, oats, and barley**
- Vegetables: **sweet potatoes, squash, potatoes**
- Beans and peas like **batong** (long beans)


**4. List of vegetables I'm Good With**

- Leafy greens: 
	- **kangkong** (water spinach)
	- **malunggay** (moringa tree)
	- **pechay** (bok choy)
	- **kamote tops**
	- **saluyot** (jute leaves)
	- **alugbati** (malabar spinach)
	- **mustasa** (mustard greens), 
- Gourds/squashes: **ampalaya** (bitter gourd), **kalabasa** (squash)
- Beans: **sitaw/batong** (long beans), **monggo** (mung beans), 
- Root crops and others: 
	- **sweet potato**
	- **potato**
	- **eggplant**
	- **tomato**
	- **chili peppers**
	- **ginger, onion and garlic**


# Foods to Eat in Low Amounts

**1. Refined Sugars and Sweets**

- **Cookies, candies, and soda** increases the risk of **metabolic diseases** when eaten in excess


**2. Fried and Processed Foods**

- **French fries, fried chicken, and chips** are high in calories, but contain unhealthy fats and excessive sodium, which can contribute to **inflammation**, **heart disease**, and weight gain in the wrong way.
