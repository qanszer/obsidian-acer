### May 30 - "More Than a Sunday Thing"

**Text:** 2 Timothy 3:16–17 (ESV)
**Goal:** Call disciples to daily Bible reading

_"Real quick — be honest. When was the last time you opened your Bible? Not in church. Not in this group. On your own."_

*"No need to answer. Isipin niyo lang"*
(Pause)

_"No judgment. I've been there too. But I want to show you tonight why it actually matters — and I think the data alone will surprise you."_

---

_"Here's the thing about Sunday service — and I love Sunday services — but here's the thing."_

- **Sunday service = processed food.** In a way na minuya na siya, na process na siya ng pastor. (pause). It keeps you alive, but you can't thrive on it alone.
- **The Bible = fresh food.** Directly from the source, fresh, 

**Question:**

_"If you could only eat processed food every week — like instant noodles, every single day — what would eventually happen to your body?"_

_(Let them answer, then bridge:)_ "The same thing happens spiritually when the only Word you get is once a week."

*"Now I'm not saying that Sunday services are bad and you should skip it altogether kasi processed food siya. Wala lang akong better analogy kaya ganyan yan hahaha. Pero gets naman yung point diba?"*

*"Sunday services are great, pero if you want to get closer to God and have a personal relationship with Him, na directly Sakanya, walang nasa gitna, then start reading the Bible on your own."* 

---

**Read it together:**

> _"All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness, so that the man of God may be complete, equipped for every good work." — 2 Timothy 3:16–17 (ESV)_

**Quick context:** Paul is writing to Timothy — a young leader, probably feeling inadequate, probably surrounded by people pulling him in different directions. Sound familiar?

Paul's answer to Timothy wasn't _"pray more"_ or _"try harder."_ It was: **get into the Word.**

**The verse tells us four uses of the Bible:**

- **Teaching** — it tells you what's true
- **Reproof** — it shows you where you're wrong
- **Correction** — it shows you how to get right
- **Training in righteousness** — it builds the habit of living right

_"Notice it doesn't say Scripture is useful for one of those things. It says all four. The Bible isn't just for when you're lost — it's for when you think you're fine too."_

**Question:** (give 30 seconds to think)

> _"Which of those four — teaching, reproof, correction, or training — do you think you need most right now, and why?"_

---

Now bring in the survey data. Deliver it like you're genuinely amazed — because you should be.

Link to the actual research: 
https://bttbfiles.com/web/docs/cbe/Scientific_Evidence_for_the_Power_of_4.pdf

> _A survey of 40,000 people looked at what happened when people read their Bible based on how many days a week.
> 
> The 1 to 3 group? Basically no difference from people who don't read at all._
> 
> _But the 4 to 7 group:_
> 
> - _30% drop in loneliness_
> - _32% less anger_
> - _42% less bitterness in relationships_
> - _61% less porn use_
> - _60% less feeling spiritually dry_

Then pause. Let it land.

_"And hindi siya Bible study program or anything. Nagbasa lang talaga sila ng Bible."_

**Question:**

> _"Looking at that list — loneliness, anger, bitterness — which one hits closest to home for you or someone you know?"_

---

_Here's what I want you to walk away with. God didn't give us the Bible as homework. He gave it to us as a lifeline. Paul told Timothy — this young guy navigating life, trying to figure life out — 'that the Word will equip you.'**_

_Nasa late teens na kayo. You're already making decisions that will shape your 20s. Who you spend time with. What you give your attention to. What you believe about yourself. So I'm proud of you guys to choose to commit your time in a dgroup and gain wisdom from God_

**Ending of the devotional**
- Start reading the Bible on your own

**Practical Advice for Starting**
- Go straight to the heart of the Bible: Jesus
	- **If you want a quick, exciting start:** Read **Mark**.
	- **If you want a comprehensive story:** Read **Luke**, followed by its sequel, **Acts**.
	- **If you want teachings about morality:** Start with **Matthew**.
	- **If you want deep spiritual reflection:** Start with **John**


---

_Total time: ~7 minutes + discussion. Adjust pacing based on how much the group engages with the questions._
