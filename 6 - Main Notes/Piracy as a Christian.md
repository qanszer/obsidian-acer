# My Convictions on Piracy as a Christian

One night, I was watching a romcom movie called 'Palm Springs' with my girlfriend and my older cousin, kuya Noynoy, reprimanded me on why I am watching a movie on a piracy streaming website when it was discussed on sunday service a few weeks ago that we should not steal as Christians. So after the movie ended, I researched about it and ended up reading the following links below on the references section.

This experience made me have the following as my new convictions:

1. It is wrong to download, read, or watch anything that should be paid for or do not own. This includes the following:
	- Downloading paid apps for free (e.g. APKs or cracked softwares). Alternative option: look for similar apps that are free, even if the performance is not as great as the paid apps (e.g. OnlyOffice instead of MicrosoftOffice)
	- Watching movies or anime for free, and reading manga or light/web novels for free. Alternative option: watch on ad-supported streaming sites, but use brave browser or similar legal ad-blocking software.
2. I have the right, or should have the right, to preserve anything I paid for legally, from unjust loss (e.g. Netflix removing a movie from their services). Piracy comes in here to maintain the status quo. Just like how the creators' pay compensations should not be stolen from them, our payment of various media should also not be stolen from us. The Bible's law of not stealing protects consumers just as much as it protects creators.

The romcom movie was great btw. 10/10. Not for below 18 years old though.
