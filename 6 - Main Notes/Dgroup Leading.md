# Dgroup Leading


## Get-To-Know Casual Questions

No pressure, no wrong answers.

1. How should we call you, and what is one fun fact about you?
2. What school are you from, and what grade are you in?
3. How did you hear about this D-Group or our church?
4. What do you enjoy doing outside of school — hobbies, interests, anything?

## Introduction to Devotional Groups

So, a D-Group — or Devotional Group — is basically a small group of people who get together regularly to study the Bible, pray together, and do life with each other. It's not a class and it's not a Sunday service — it's more like a safe space where you can ask real questions, be honest about what you're going through, and actually grow in your faith with people who are walking the same road. Think of it like iron sharpening iron — we're here to help each other become more like Christ, not to have it all figured out. It's casual, it's real, and it's for you. And everything shared will only be between us. It will be confidential.

## Vulnerability Sharing

I want to be upfront with you. I'm a complete beginner at leading a devotional group. I have anxiety about this, and honestly, I fear I won't lead you as well as an established group could. But here's why I'm still doing this: My life isn't mine anymore. It belongs to God.

Years ago, I attempted suicide. Everything felt hopeless. I was standing on a cliff, ready to jump into the ocean. But the only thing that came to my mind—the _only_ thing—was Psalm 23. Words I'd memorized from church flowed out, and I broke down crying. God stopped me that day. He gave me a second life.

I'm here because I want to share that love with you. Not because I have it all figured out. But because I've experienced His faithfulness when I had nothing else.

So yes, I'll mess up. Yes, I won't know all the answers. But I'll be real with you, and I'll seek truth together with you. That's what this group is.

## Interview/Screening Questions

- "Tell me about your faith journey—how did you come to know Jesus?"
- "What are you hoping to get out of a group like this?"
- "Are you ready to be honest here—to share struggles, doubts, questions?"
- "Can you commit to showing up consistently?"
- "What areas of your life do you want God to transform right now?"

Make them feel heard. Ask follow-ups. Remember details. Show genuine interest, not just recruitment.

## Dgroup Details

- 15 minutes of genuine fellowship (icebreakers, games, food)
- **Introduction** (2-3 min): Hook with a relatable question or story
- **Need** (2-3 min): Connect the struggle to their real lives
- **Text** (10-15 min): Deep study using your hermeneutics framework
- **Application** (5-7 min): Specific, attainable steps for this week
- **Challenge** (2-3 min): End with conviction to act
- Questions
- Prayer Requests

 **Reminder**: Devotionals should force reflection, not yes/no answers. Your goal is they leave thinking "I know exactly what to do."
