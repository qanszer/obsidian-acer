## **DAY 1: JOB 1-2** (The Setup)

### **PRAY FIRST:**

_"God, I'm starting the book of Job. Help me understand why this book is in the Bible and what You want to teach me through Job's story. Give me insight and keep me attentive. In Jesus' name, Amen."_

### **READ: Job 1-2**

### **SUMMARY:**

**Chapter 1:**

- Job is introduced as a righteous, wealthy man who fears God and avoids evil
- He has 10 children and is the greatest man in the East
- **Scene shifts to heaven:** Satan appears before God
- God points out Job's righteousness
- Satan challenges: "Job only serves You because You bless him. Take away his blessings and he'll curse You."
- God permits Satan to test Job but not harm him physically
- **Job loses everything in one day:** his livestock, servants, and all 10 children die
- **Job's response:** "The LORD gave, and the LORD has taken away. Blessed be the name of the LORD." (v. 21)
- Key point: **Job did not sin or charge God with wrongdoing**

**Chapter 2:**

- Satan appears before God again
- Satan says: "Job only remained faithful because he's still healthy. Touch his body and he'll curse You."
- God permits Satan to afflict Job with painful sores from head to foot
- Job's wife says: "Curse God and die!"
- **Job's response:** "Shall we accept good from God and not trouble?" (v. 10)
- Key point: **Job still did not sin in what he said**
- Three friends arrive: Eliphaz, Bildad, and Zophar
- They sit with Job in silence for 7 days because his suffering is so great

**KEY INSIGHTS:**

- Job is **innocent**â€”God Himself declares Job righteous (1:8)
- Job's suffering is **not punishment for sin**â€”it's a test permitted by God
- Job's friends **don't know about the conversation in heaven**â€”they assume Job sinned
- This sets up the whole book: Job maintains his innocence while his friends insist he must have sinned

### **REFLECT:**

_"What does Job's response teach me about trusting God even when life falls apart?"_

## **DAY 3: JOB 4-5** (Eliphaz's First Speech)

### **PRAY FIRST:**

_"God, as I read Eliphaz's speech, help me discern what is true and what is false. Help me see where Job's friends misrepresent You. Amen."_

### **READ: Job 4-5**

### **SUMMARY:**

Eliphaz is the first friend to respond to Job's lament. He represents **traditional religious wisdom**â€”but he's WRONG.

**Eliphaz's main argument:**

**Chapter 4:**

- "Think backâ€”does the innocent person perish? When have the upright been destroyed?" (4:7)
- His logic: **Suffering = punishment for sin**
- "Those who plow evil and sow trouble reap it" (4:8)
- He describes a vision he had (4:12-21) where a spirit told him no one is righteous before God
- Implication: "Job, you must have sinned. Admit it."

**Chapter 5:**

- "Blessed is the one whom God corrects" (5:17)
- He's saying: "This is discipline. Just repent and God will restore you."
- He promises that if Job repents, God will heal and bless him again
- Ends with: "We have examined this, and it is true" (5:27)

**WHY ELIPHAZ IS WRONG:**

1. **His theology is too simple:** Suffering â‰  always punishment
    
    - Jesus Himself said the man born blind wasn't blind because of sin (John 9:3)
    - Righteous people DO suffer (persecution, testing, spiritual warfare)
2. **God explicitly told us Job is INNOCENT** (chapters 1-2)
    
    - Eliphaz doesn't know about the heavenly conversation
    - He's operating on assumptions, not truth
3. **God rebukes Eliphaz at the end** (42:7)
    
    - God says: "You have not spoken the truth about me, as my servant Job has"
    - Eliphaz's view of God is WRONG

**ELIPHAZ'S MISTAKE:** He takes a **partial truth** (sin does have consequences) and makes it an **absolute rule** (all suffering is punishment). This is bad theology.

**KEY INSIGHT:** Just because someone sounds religious and quotes spiritual truths doesn't mean they're right. **Job's friends represent well-meaning but wrong theology.** Their speeches are in the Bible to show us what NOT to think about suffering.

## **DAY 5: JOB 19** (Job's Declaration of Faith)

### **PRAY FIRST:**

_"God, in the middle of Job's suffering, he makes an incredible declaration of faith. Open my eyes to see this hope. Amen."_

### **READ: Job 19**

### **SUMMARY:**

By chapter 19, Job has been through **two full rounds** of speeches from his friends. They keep accusing him. Job keeps defending himself. And now, in his pain, Job makes one of the most powerful declarations of faith in all of Scripture.

**First part: Job's complaint (v. 1-22)**

- "God has wronged me" (v. 6)

- "Have pity on me, my friends, have pity, for the hand of God has struck me" (v. 21)

**THENâ€”THE TURNING POINT (v. 23-27):**

> _"I know that my Redeemer lives,_  
> _and that in the end he will stand on the earth._

> _yet in my flesh I will see God;_

**WHAT THIS MEANS:**

1. **"My Redeemer lives"**

2. **"In the end he will stand on the earth"**
    
    - Job has hope beyond this life
    - Even if he dies in this suffering, his Redeemer will come
3. **"After my skin has been destroyed, yet in my flesh I will see God"**
    
    - This is an early hint at **bodily resurrection**
    - Job believes that even after death, he will physically see God and be vindicated
4. **"I myself will see him with my own eyes"**
    
    - Personal, direct encounter with God
    - Job's ultimate hope isn't just explanationâ€”it's seeing God face-to-face

**KEY INSIGHTS:**

    - He doesn't understand what's happening

## **DAY 6: JOB 38-39** (God Speaks - Part 1)

### **PRAY FIRST:**

_"Lord, You're about to speak to Job. Help me hear Your voice with humility. Show me who You are through Your words to Job. Amen."_

### **READ: Job 38-39**

### **SUMMARY:**

**Chapter 38: God's Questions About Creation**

**KEY INSIGHT:**

**God's answer to suffering is not EXPLANATIONâ€”it's HIMSELF.**

Job wanted to know WHY. God says: "You don't need to know why. You need to know WHO I am."

**The God who:**

- Controls the stars
- Knows the depths of the sea
- Gives life to animals
- Manages complexity you can't even comprehend

**Trust His character, not your understanding.**

## **OVERALL LESSONS FROM JOB**

### **1. Suffering is not always punishment for sin**

- Job was righteous, yet suffered terribly
- Don't judge others' pain as deserved

### **2. We won't always understand why we suffer**

- God never told Job about the heavenly conversation
- We see "through a glass, darkly" (1 Cor 13:12)

### **3. God's answer to suffering is HIMSELF, not EXPLANATION**

- Job wanted to know WHY
- God showed him WHO
- Knowing God is enough

### **4. Honest lament is biblical**

- Bring your real pain to God
- Wrestling with God is not the same as rejecting God

### **5. Bad theology hurts people**

- Job's friends had wrong views of God and suffering
- Beware of simplistic formulas for complex situations

### **6. Trust God's character when you can't see His plan**

- "I know my Redeemer lives"
- Faith doesn't require full understanding

### **7. God is sovereign over all things**

- Even Satan had to ask permission to test Job
- Nothing happens outside God's control

### **8. There's a bigger spiritual reality we don't see**

- The conversation in heaven (chapters 1-2)
- Our struggles are part of a cosmic story
