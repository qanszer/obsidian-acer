## **02 - TEXT**

Hindi lang siya good life advice—**it's how Jesus himself acted.** 

### **Jesus is actually the Master Affirmer**
He is a master at the skill of seeing the good in others.

For example in **Matthew 9:9** - When Jesus called Matthew, someone infamous as a despised tax collector, He didn't lead with: _"Matthew, you're a thief and a traitor to your people."_

Instead, Jesus saw potential. He said, **"Follow Me."** And Matthew left everything to follow Him.

Also in **John 1:42** - When Jesus first met Simon Peter, He didn't say: _"You're impulsive, you're unstable, you're going to deny Me three times in the future."_  but instead, 

Jesus said: **"You are Simon... but you will be called Cephas (Peter)" —which means 'rock.'** Jesus looked at a man who was anything BUT rock-solid and affirmed that he would become one. And he did.

May nakikita ba kayong pattern in how to be an affirmer based on the two examples? 

Actually, connected din siya sa main theme ng last IDC, which is that God uses broken people. 

Jesus didn't wait for us to get it right. **He affirmed His love for us while we were still a mess.**

**And we have the power to do the same for others.** Hindi siya with hypothetical or prophetic words of what they could become, but with their own history of goodness, of success, of insight, na nakita mo sa kanila. Their existing qualities na kaya maggrow. 

### **The Biblical Principle**

There are countless verses in the Bible that support this, one of them being **Ephesians 4:29** which says:

> _"Do not let any unwholesome talk come out of your mouths, but only what is helpful for building others up according to their needs, that it may benefit those who listen."_

Notice: **"building others up"**

If magbbuild ka, kailangan mo ng existing materials--aka existing qualities--that can be built up into something greater. (interaction)


There's a brilliant quote that captures this:

_"When we treat man as he is, we make him worse than he is; but when we treat him as if he already were what he potentially could be, we make him what he should be."_

Or for easier understanding, if we treat people as if they've already reached the potential of what they could be in the future, then we are influencing them of what they would become in the future.


**Jesus did this constantly.** Nag inspire siya ng fishermen to instead be "fishers of men." And to the man na alam niyang iddeny siya 3 times in the future, he named him Peter, which means "rock", something that does not budge.

**Basically, He saw the good in other people—even when it was buried under layers of brokenness—and He spoke it into existence.**

### **Second Application: When someone disappoints you, surprise them with grace**

Eto yung mahirap.

For example, **someone during live messes up. Or you had high expectations on someone's performance, but they ended up disappointing you. Or someone makes you mad because they keep making the same mistake, over and over again na para bang di sila nakinig sa sinabi mo.**

Yung instinct natin usually is to point it out and correct them harshly, maybe even vent about it. 

**But here's the golden opportunity that many people miss:** What if instead, you responded with **undeserved grace and affirmation** to them?

(pause)

Pero if you really do need to correct them, do the sandwich method:

- bun - affirm/appreciate 
- burger - correct/rebuke 
- bun - affirm/appreciate

**Romans 5:8** says:

> _"But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us."_

Again, Jesus didn't wait for us to get it right. **He affirmed His love for us while we were still a mess.**

**And we have the power to do the same for others.**


Here's the truth: **Jesus did not wait for us to be perfect before He called us His own.**

**So let's not wait for others to be perfect before we affirm the good in them.**
