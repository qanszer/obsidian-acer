## Defining "State"

> "State is independent political communities each of which possesses a government and asserts sovereignty about a particular portion of Earth's surface and a particular segment of a human population." — *Hedley Bull*

## Defining "Interstate"

> "A system of unequally powerful and competing states in which no single state is capable of imposing control on others. These states are in interaction with one another in a set of shifting alliances and wars, and change in relative power of states upsets any temporary set of alliances, leading to a restructuring of balance and power."

---

## What is the Global Interstate System?

Also commonly called the **international system**, it is the main focus of the field of International Relations. It is a network that connects various countries and regions around the world.

**Purpose:** To facilitate the movement of people, goods, and services from one place to another, resulting in faster economic and social development.

---

## Brief History

- **Ancient Civilizations** — origins of interstate relations
- **Treaty of Westphalia (1648)** — established state sovereignty principles
- **19th–20th centuries** — shaped by imperialism, World Wars, and decolonization; emergence of new states and power dynamics
- **Post-World War II** — formation of the United Nations and international organizations aimed at promoting peace and cooperation
- **Today** — characterized by diplomacy, trade, and cooperation among sovereign states; continues to evolve amidst geopolitical tensions and global issues

## Globalization and the Nation-States

**Max Weber** (German social theorist) defined the state as a compulsory political organization with a centralized government that maintains a monopoly of the legitimate use of force within a certain territory.

**Hedley Bull** (20th-century international philosopher) stated that states are independent political communities each of which possesses a government and asserts sovereignty in relation to a particular portion of the earth's surface and a particular segment of the human population.

Globalization in the early years of the 21st century has **not displaced the state**.

## The State and Economic Interdependence

The rising momentum of global free-market capitalism in the final decades of the 20th century, the accompanying rise in transnational enterprises, and the resulting disparities between easy flows of money and commodities across international borders and the legal barriers and logistical hurdles that keep most workers tied to their home communities are all associated with **globalization**.

---

## Neoliberalism

Neoliberalism is the intensification of the influence and dominance of capital. It is the elevation of capitalism as a mode of production into an ethics, a set of political imperatives, and a cultural logic. It is a project to strengthen, restore, or, in some cases, constitute anew the power of economic elites.

## Economic Sovereignty

Economic sovereignty is the power of national governments to make decisions independently of those made by other governments. Globalization — as an increase in the international integration of markets for goods, services, capital, and labor — is also a counterpoint of national sovereignty.

### Types of Sovereignty
1. **International Legal Sovereignty**
2. **Westphalian Sovereignty**
3. **Interdependence Sovereignty**
4. **Domestic Sovereignty**
5. **Economic Sovereignty**

> **Key Question:** In a highly globalized world, is economic sovereignty still achievable?

## Effects of Globalization in Local Government

### Community Impact
- **Community Exposure** — communities are exposed to global forces and changes
- **Property Values** — globalization influences local real estate and development
- **Jobs & Opportunities** — creates new employment while potentially displacing existing industries

**In Reality:** As the world becomes more interconnected through politics, trade, and communications, the role of states and governments is also shifting.

---

## Institutions that Govern Globalization

With growing globalization, governance relations go beyond national governments. One country's scale and scope are limited in addressing regional and global issues like climate change, cybercrimes, global financial crises, and territorial disputes.

### Key International Organizations
- **United Nations (UN)**
- **World Trade Organization (WTO)**
- **International Monetary Fund (IMF)**
- **World Bank**

---

## Conclusion

The global interstate system reflects the complex and evolving relationships between sovereign states in an increasingly interconnected world. Globalization has not eliminated the state, but has shifted and complicated its role — requiring greater cooperation through international institutions while raising important questions about sovereignty, economic independence, and global governance.
