# Another Death Sentence

On the 14th of June 1830, US President Andrew Jackson granted paron to convicted robber George Wilson who was handed the death sentence by the circuit cort of the eastern district of Pennsylvania. This means that the robber is free from his crime and does not need to pay the consequence of his robbery.

However, on October 21, 1830, George Wilson appeared in court and said that "he had nothing to say, and that he did not wish in any manner to avail himself of the pardon referred to." Sadly, even if pardon from the death sentence was available for Mr. Wilson, "the court cannot give the prisoner the benefit of the pardon, unless he claims the benefit of it, and relies on it by plea or motion... It is a grant to him... he may accept it or not, as he pleases."

---

This story can be reflected on the gift of salvation that God has given us through Jesus Christ's death on the cross, for our sins. He made a way for us to be saved and be with Him eternally, but it is ultimately our choice if we want to accept His gift of salvation.
