# Study of the Gospel


## What is the Reason for the Gospel?

**Sinful Nature of Man**

- Satan wants us to believe that we are good enough. And that not all sins are bad. 
- The separation from God is caused by our sin--our iniquities

**Sin is Rebellion**

- It is the pride in our hearts saying we can be like God and know better than Him
- It makes us enemies of God, which its payment is death

**Sin is Separation**

- Our sinful nature separates us from God
- Do we take sin seriously?
- What motivates you to lead people?

Make your disciples dependent to God, not to you

Our role is to show Jesus in our everyday lives.


**Our sinful nature prevents us from saying not to sin and makes us blind to God, not realizing the need for a savior.**

The reason of the Gospel is the response of God to sin, to bring us back to Him.


## What is the Gospel?

- It is the story of the Gospel is the love of God made manifest in the form of Christ
- It is the love of God reaching out to us wile we were yet sinners, that we may be with Him in Heaven
- Christ has died, Christ has risen, and Christ will come again


**Reconciliation with God**


**Lamb of God**


The Cross of Christ is the symbol of God's love for sinful man to one day be reconciled with Him.


**When you lead a dgroup, you are considered your disciples' shepherd, their guide, their pastor. You have to know them personally, their struggles, in order to share Jesus to them effectively.**


**Effect of the Gospel**

- It points us to the life of Jesus


## What is the Purpose of the Gospel?

Simple. It is to bring us to hope. The true hope.

**The Offer of Salvation**

- God in His love sacrifised His son Jesus to deliver us from the penalty of Sin
- We have the power to decide if we want to accept Him or not. The same with other people. That is why you cannot force them. It is their decision ultimately.

**Strengthen our Faith**

- Building a relationship with God is important. Because God's answers to our prayers is yes, no, and wait. Being able to handle when God says no is something that someone who has a personal relationship with Him can do.

Let us not walk at people as walking problems. Let us look at them with compassion because God can save them. Nothing is impossible with God. 

Do not say to any person that they have no hope, na wala silang pag-asa. Because God can save them. And it is in our actions that they will be able to tell that God is capable of doing that.


The Gospel is the Good News of salvation that Jesus preached that we may walk iwth the confidence and assurance of our faith in HIm. Trusting in His power by the

# Evangelism as a Lifestyle

People crave for truth. Especially the youth of today's generation.

So how can we make evangelism part of our life?


## 01 - Head of Evangelism

According to Matthew 28:18-20 (The Great Commission), it's our **identity** as Christians to go out and share the Gospel.

Sent people do not stay, they go.


## 02 - Heart of Evangelism

**Compassion = splagchnizomai**
It comes from *Splagchna*, which refers to the inner organs (bowels, intenstines, heart area). In ancient Jewish and Greek thought, this area was considered the **seat of deep emotions**.

**Evangelism begins in compassion.**


## 03 - Hands of Evangelism

*"Can I pray for you? Wala lang, I just felt like you need it."*

Gospel sharing can happen in a normal conversation. It is incorporating it into our daily lives.

#### Pray, Care, Share

1. **Namestorm**
	Listing down of names God impressed into your heart
	- Contact numbers
	- Social media
	- Neighbors
	- Workmates
	- Classmates
2. **Pray**
	- Pray for an open heart
	- Pray for opportunities
	- Pray for a surrendered heart
	- Pray for more names
3. **Care**
	- Find common interests
	- Treat them some coffee, lunch, etc
	- Be intentional in asking for prayer requests
	- Be the salt and light to them
4. **Share**
	- Sometimes we stop in the care section because we feel like we already showed Jesus to them. Even then, do not make shortcuts
	- Introduce Jesus to them
	- Be sensitive to the Holy Spirit's leading


> 
> **Not until we do it again and again, evangelism will not be incorporated into our lives.**
> 

#### Extra Points

1. Encourage questions
2. Do not wait for the perfect opportunity
3. Be Christ-like in all situations
4. See all conversations as a Gospel conversation
	- Be ready to share how Jesus changed your life

# Discipling vs Mentoring

**Similarities**

- Committing time and effort with at least 2 people
- Meets for a purpose and work out results
- Improvement

|              | Discipling                                                                                                       | Mentoring                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Duration     | Long term                                                                                                        | Short to long term                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Focus        | Spiritual growth; Jesus Christ as model                                                                          | Personal growth; professional, moral, spiritual                                                                                                                                                 |
| Approach     | Clarifies, directs to Scriptures                                                                                 | Clarifies, directs, supports                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Relationship | Hierarchical, moral authority                                                                                    | Relational, informal                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Benchmark    | Faith and values                                                                                                 | Current position                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Purpose      | The focus is spiritual growth and developing closer relationship with Christ. The goal is to be more Christ-like | The focus is of a broader personal development (professional, financial, emotional etc). The goal is to provide wisdom, guidance and encouragement to help achieve goals or navigate challenges |
| Content      |                                                                                                                  |                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
> 
> **Mentoring** - Equipping for wholistic growth; a much larger scope than discipling
> 
> **Discipling** - Focused on evangelism, accountability, transformation and multiplication
> 

For CCF, it encourages both wherein there is a cohesive relationship between the two
