# TOP Success Stories


### Remote Junior Developer / Data Engineer - PH
[Link](https://discord.com/channels/505093832157691914/1463004166908543142)

I graduated as a Computer Science. But my life as a CS student was full of clutter, I don't know what I wan't, I don't know what to do, and I don't know what projects I want to build. As a CS student you have been pressured by the Internet to build stuffs to improve your resume. But for someone like me who is lost in the in the Degree I have chosen. I didn't know I would really like doing something Web Development until I tried The Odin Project. When my school teach me about theories, I spend my time in TOP doing projects that makes me more comfortable in developing things. I think school should properly teach their students how to be developer with instruction to set-up their PC/Laptop to be ready for the Industry. If I were to compare things that I've learned that I have used in my job, 90% of it is from The Odin Project.

After graduation I gain a lot of Web Development skills. To test this skill I learned 'initation', I tried to learn Mobile Development and see if I can do it, and yes with the things I learned in TOP. all those 'boring' MDN docs that you have to read through, I have learned the important experience of navigating through documentation, believe me there is lots of developers out there who is lacking in these skills, and I wish Universities would teach that to improve the quality of developers today. Mobile Development was 'easy' to learn since I am not afraid to navigate those things. Ohh I know this is similar to Web Dev, what is the equivalent of this in Mobile Development. and so on questions. Even I tried learning Python and diving with Data Engineering. My intuition in navigating docs was really helpful that I gained a lot of skills in the process. That makes me unique in applying various jobs.

In JS world, programmers there like to do things modularized, or having to decide on which library to use, given example is express, in express you don't have DB/Orms, you have to either decide to use something like Prisma/Drizzle/TypeORM, and for validation tool maybe use Zod or express validator. In Ruby World, it seems to be frowned upon on doing things like in the JS World. There is already RoR which have everything set-up to you including the ORMs and validation tool. Well ruby has Sinatra which is similar to Express but it is becoming rare nowadays.

For Jobs related, it really depends on your Area, here in the PH we are now starting to ditch express now in favor of its opinionated version NestJS (check this out too!)

Sadly these certificates that I received from them I believe, HR don't bother much or see value in it in Web Dev positions, Unless you do like Data Engineering where credentials matters a lot then yes those conferences maybe useful. But If thats me, I would maximize Projects. Since during an HR interview that is where you really speaks a lot (if you don't have exp yet).

Currently I am doing remote position, main office is based on Cavite, I would say its becoming a new normal for these kind of work, I felt like there is more remote job offers than onsite now. Although from what I heard from my classmates and whom are doing on-site jobs. There is always an IT Hub like Cebu, BGC, and Manila companies that would like to recruit for onsite jobs and its plenty there

One advice I got is that its more direct to get these offers through facebook groups. So if you are close to these places you might have some advantage. I can say its 'some' because most of HR interviews will be done online nowadays. And they will always ask at the last phase of the HR round if you are willing to move or you need assistance. For these companies they will probably more likely to hire someone who is closer than those who are far since its cheaper and they don't have to spend that money to move a new employee if the position is On-site otherwise they couldn't care that much.

On Facebook groups, is it possible to elaborate more? Do you mean like job postings? Or are employers hiring using FB groups? 

Both actually, I found some people have success by posting their resume there. some employers post and dm every replies with resume

there are some elective subjects where I excel at because of TOP like Software Engineering, and Application Architecture which involve a lot of deploying your app to production, I did a lot of my app demo by deploying to free cloud infa like render, vercel, supabase, and aivenDB which I wouldn't know otherwise if I didn't take TOP (I also contribute here, you can check it from the Deployment lesson)

Web Dev skills is still going to be very useful because there are some knowledge that is common to other specialization like Database, Designing Software, Version Control, etc. 

And also Web is basically the common middleground in Internet, you are still going to host AI models behind a Backend API, Crypto service, finance service, and many more. Basically you can't lose with Web Development knowledge
