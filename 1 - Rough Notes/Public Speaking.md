
  

1. Enter like you own the room

2. Act like all of this is natural to you

3. Act like you own your time

4. Take your time in your small actions

5. Do smooth and comfortable actions

6. Know your subject well (most important)

7. Have cadence in your speech

8. Keep your last syllable's tone down and deep, for more persuasion

9. Implement pauses in the way you talk

10. Mimic great speakers and communicators in how they talk

11. Remember that you are not changing yourself, you are only abiding by what is expected of you in a professional sense

12. When speaking in public, choose 3 people in different parts of the audience, and only look at them or in their direction. Talking and explaining to 3 people is easier than doing so to all of the audience.

13. Own your temporal space

14. Be gentle to yourself when you make mistakes

15. Mistakes are for learning; they are not failures
